Kidney stone treatment bias

Pulkit Kulshrestha - pkulshr1@asu.edu

Kidney stone treatment is a medical condition that can be influenced by various factors such as age, gender, type and size of the stone, and other underlying health conditions. Simpson's paradox is a statistical phenomenon that occurs when a trend appears in different groups of data, but disappears or reverses when these groups are combined. In the context of kidney stone treatment, Simpson's paradox could arise if one treatment(A) appears to be more effective than another when comparing different subgroups of patients, but when considering the whole population, the opposite is true(B). For example, if we compare the success rates of two different kidney stone treatments in small and large stone patients separately, we may find that one treatment is more effective in each group.
